Intro

Hi there! My name is Thomas Wonderley.

I am a Postgraduate Researcher in Nuclear Instrumentation working in the Department of Physics at the University of Liverpool. My research focusses on using Artificial Intelligence to improve the performance and reliability of HPGe detectors. This project is part of the LIV.INNO centre for doctoral training in data intesive science.

I completed my Masters degree in Physics at the University of Liverpool in 2022, in which I focussed my dissertation on investigating the nuclear structure of Cerium-132 using gamma ray coincidence measurements taken from the GAMMASPHERE detector array.

Hobbies & Interests

Outside of work, I enjoy being active and outdoors including hiking, bouldering and sport climbing. For the last fewy years I have been part of the Liverpool University Mountaineering Club, during which I was the treasurer of the club for two years. I am an avid hiker, and have completed many of the Wainwright peaks in the Lake District, as well as a number of peaks in the Peak District and Wales. I have also visited climbing crags all across the UK as well as Fontainbleau in France, and El Chorro in the south of Spain for bouldering and sport climbing, respectively.

Other than this, I enjoy singing and playing instruments, mainly the guitar although I have dabbled in the drums and the bass, and I sing in a professional duet with my partner.
